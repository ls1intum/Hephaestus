Hephaestus turns observations from your pull requests, issues, and reviews into practice reflections instead of scores. There is no leaderboard, no points, and no ranking anywhere – the goal is to help you see your own habits and act on them.

## My Practices

Your workspace home shows **My Practices**: one reflection card per engineering practice observed in your recent work. Each card explains

- **why the practice matters** and what good looks like,
- **where you currently stand**, based on your own pull requests, issues, and reviews,
- **specific feedback to act on**, plus the things you already do well.

These cards are private to you. They reorganise the same feedback you receive on your pull requests by practice, so you can reflect at your own pace instead of hunting through review threads. Use the **Copy my practice summary** button to copy your cards as text – handy for a retrospective, a mentoring session, or your own notes. Nothing on this page blocks your work in any way.

:::info Yours alone
My Practices is about your own work. Cards describe observations against practice criteria for the current review cycle, and every practice is judged on its own terms.
:::

## Practice Overview (mentors and administrators)

Workspace administrators and owners have a **Practice Overview** page to support mentoring:

- **Workspace health** – for each practice area, how many developers stand at each status (Strength, Focus area, or Mixed). Administrators and owners always see the counts. When the view is shared with all members, it is anonymised for them: a card appears to members once five or more developers are active in that area, so nobody can be singled out. An area with no recent activity shows as having no data yet, which is not the same thing.
- **Roster** – a named list of developers with activity, sorted so people who may need attention come first (then by login). The order is a triage aid: it brings people a mentor could help to the top.
- **Drill-down** – a mentor can open an individual developer's practice cards (the same cards that developer sees) to prepare a conversation.

Both the roster and the practice cards also show whether a practice is improving or declining since the last review cycle, so conversations can start from trajectory.

### Access is controlled and audited

- The named roster and the per-developer drill-down are restricted to workspace **administrators and owners**. Every time either is viewed, Hephaestus records an append-only audit entry (who accessed whose report, and when) as a compliance record.
- The anonymised workspace health view is visible to mentors only by default (the **workspace health visibility** setting). A workspace administrator can choose to share it with **all members** – even then, it stays aggregated and small groups remain suppressed.

Questions about how this data is handled? See the privacy statement linked in the app footer.
