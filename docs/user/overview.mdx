**How You Build Matters.** Hephaestus analyzes how your team collaborates — detecting anti-patterns, coaching contributors, and tracking improvement over time. Currently grounded in GitHub activity (PRs, reviews, commits), it delivers feedback through leaderboards, an AI mentor (Heph), and automated practice checks. Whether you're onboarding into your first project or contributing across multiple repositories, the tools below help you understand and improve your practices.

![Hephaestus hero illustration](./img/overview/agile_hephaestus.png)

This guide is tailored for learners, mentors, and course staff who work inside the production environment at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de). You will find practical instructions for daily workflows as well as tips for getting the most value out of Hephaestus.

## What Hephaestus offers

### Recognition & engagement

- **Weekly leaderboard** with real-time updates across your team's projects.
- **Team competitions** and **leagues** that reward consistent, high-quality contributions.
- **Automated recognition** via Slack digests, spotlighting top contributors and notable work each week.

### AI mentor (Heph)

- Structured coaching sessions that guide you through goal-setting, progress review, and reflection.
- Grounded in your actual activity data — Heph surfaces relevant work, reviews, and action items.
- Generates shareable session summaries you can pin to your dashboard or bring to team standups.

### Practice detection

- LLM-based checks that flag specific issues in how work is documented and reviewed (e.g., missing context, unclear motivation), with suggested improvements.
- Draft PRs receive lighter checks; ready-to-merge work gets rigorous review.
- Mark findings as fixed, adjusted, or not applicable to close the loop.

:::info Workspace management
Workspace settings let admins choose which repositories to monitor, assign team roles, and control what data is sent to LLM providers.
:::

## Who should use this guide?

- Students and engineers participating in a Hephaestus-enabled course or project.
- Teaching assistants and mentors who facilitate sprint rituals and monitor learner progress.
- Researchers evaluating review activity, mentoring engagement, or gamification outcomes.

This section is exclusively for end-user workflows. For deployment, architecture, or contribution guidance, jump to the **Contributor Guide** or **Admin Guide** instead.

## Quick start

1. Sign in with your GitHub account at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de).
2. Join your course or team workspace and connect the repositories you actively review.
3. Open the **Mentor** tab to set your goals, then track progress on the **Leaderboard** each week.

Continue with [Getting started](./getting-started) for a hands-on walkthrough of the onboarding journey.
