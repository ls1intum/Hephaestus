**Mentoring feedback, grounded in your work.** Hephaestus gives developers the mentoring feedback they would otherwise only get from a mentor, so that every developer gets some. It reads the pull requests, issues, and reviews your team already writes and tells each contributor what was done well, what could be better, and a way to get there. You can act on the feedback, push back with a reason, or let it pass.

![Hephaestus hero illustration](./img/overview/agile_hephaestus.png)

This guide is tailored for learners, mentors, and course staff who work inside the production environment at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de). You will find practical instructions for daily workflows as well as tips for getting the most value out of Hephaestus.

## What Hephaestus offers

### AI practice feedback on pull requests and issues

- Reviews activity in connected GitHub and GitLab repositories against a workspace-configurable catalogue of engineering practices.
- Posts feedback as comments directly on the pull/merge request or issue, right where the work happens.
- Feedback is advisory: it names what went well, what could be better, and a way to get there.

### AI mentor (Heph)

- A chat grounded in your repository activity, so the mentor can surface relevant pull requests and action items.
- Guided conversations that support reflection on your recent work.
- Also available in Slack, right where your team talks.

### Workspace management

- Flexible repository and team configuration governed at the workspace level.
- Fine-grained access controls to keep data scoped to the teams and mentors who need it.
- Built-in privacy guardrails so learners control what information is shared beyond their teams.

### Optional recognition features

Workspace admins can additionally enable achievements and a weekly leaderboard that tracks review activity, with team filters and Slack digests. See [Leaderboard](./leaderboard) for details.

## Who should use this guide?

- Students and engineers contributing in a Hephaestus-enabled course or project.
- Teaching assistants and mentors who facilitate sprint rituals and support learner progress.
- Researchers evaluating review activity and mentoring engagement.

## Quick start

1. Sign in with your GitHub account at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de).
2. Join your course or team workspace and connect the repositories you actively work in.
3. Open the **Mentor** tab to talk to Heph, and watch for practice feedback on your next pull request.

Continue with [Getting started](./getting-started) for a hands-on walkthrough of the onboarding journey.
