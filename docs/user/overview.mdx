Each project keeps a short list of the practices that matter. When a pull request comes in, that list and the contribution are read together, and a comment appears alongside the existing review.

![Hephaestus hero illustration](./img/overview/agile_hephaestus.png)

This guide is for anyone using Hephaestus at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de) — students, mentors, course staff.

## What you'll see

**On a pull request.** A comment from Hephaestus, alongside the rest of the review. It points at one practice, with the evidence behind it and a suggested next move. Take what fits. Push back on what doesn't.

**In the mentor.** A conversation when you want one. Talk through what to focus on this week, where you're stuck, what a recent review made you think about. The mentor has access to your recent activity.

**On your profile.** Your contributions and your review activity, visible inside your workspace.

**In Slack.** An optional weekly leaderboard summary, if your admin has it on.

:::info Workspace settings
Admins choose which repositories are in scope, who plays which role, and which AI model provider Hephaestus talks to.
:::

## Who this guide is for

- Students and engineers in a course or project that uses Hephaestus.
- Teaching assistants and supervisors who watch a team's progress.

:::tip
For deployment and contribution, head to the **Contributor Guide** or the **Admin Guide**.
:::

## Get started

1. Sign in at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de) with GitHub.
2. Land in your workspace. Repositories sync from your organisation automatically.
3. Read the next finding on a pull request. Open the mentor when you want to think out loud.

Read on with [Getting started](./getting-started).
