Hephaestus evaluates contributions against the practices your team defines. When something needs attention, it tells the contributor directly — with guidance that adapts based on their track record.

![Hephaestus hero illustration](./img/overview/agile_hephaestus.png)

This guide is for anyone using Hephaestus at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de) — whether you're a student, mentor, or course staff.

## What Hephaestus offers

### Practice detection

- Admins define a **practice catalog** per workspace — your standards, not generic rules.
- An AI agent evaluates each contribution against those practices and produces **structured findings** with a verdict, severity, evidence, and tailored guidance.
- Mark findings as **applied**, **disputed**, or **not applicable** to close the loop.

### AI mentor (Heph)

- Structured coaching sessions that guide you through goal-setting, progress review, and reflection.
- Grounded in your actual activity data — Heph surfaces relevant work, reviews, and action items.
- Generates session summaries that can be shared with mentors or brought to team standups.

### Engagement & recognition

- **Weekly leaderboard** with real-time updates across your team's projects.
- **Team competitions** and **leagues** that reward consistent, high-quality contributions.
- **Automated recognition** via Slack digests, spotlighting top contributors and notable work each week.

:::info Workspace management
Workspace settings let admins choose which repositories to monitor, assign team roles, and control what data is sent to LLM providers.
:::

## Who should use this guide?

- Students and engineers participating in a Hephaestus-enabled course or project.
- Teaching assistants and mentors who monitor learner progress and review practice detection results.

:::tip Stay on track
This section is exclusively for end-user workflows. For deployment, architecture, or contribution guidance, jump to the **Contributor Guide** or **Admin Guide** instead.
:::

## Quick start

1. Sign in with your GitHub or GitLab account at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de).
2. Join your course or team workspace and connect the repositories you actively review.
3. Open the **Mentor** tab to set your goals, then track progress on the **Leaderboard** each week.

Continue with [Getting started](./getting-started) for a hands-on walkthrough of the onboarding journey.
