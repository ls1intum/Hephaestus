Hephaestus reads each pull request against the practices your project cares about, and writes back — beside the change, with examples, in language you can act on.

![Hephaestus hero illustration](./img/overview/agile_hephaestus.png)

This guide is for anyone using Hephaestus at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de) — students, mentors, course staff.

## What you'll see

**On a pull request.** A comment from Hephaestus, alongside the rest of the review. It points at the change, says what's working or what to try, and ends with a small concrete next step. Mark it as something you've taken on, something you'd push back on, or not relevant.

**In the mentor.** A conversation when you want one. Talk through what to focus on this week, where you're stuck, what a recent review made you think about. The mentor knows what you've been working on.

**On your profile.** A picture of your week — what you've contributed, how your practices are trending. Yours alone.

**In Slack.** An optional weekly note from your workspace, if your admin has it on.

:::info Workspace settings
Admins choose which repositories are in scope, who plays which role, and which model provider Hephaestus talks to.
:::

## Who this guide is for

- Students and engineers in a course or project that uses Hephaestus.
- Teaching assistants and supervisors who watch a team's progress.

:::tip
For deployment and contribution, head to the **Contributor Guide** or the **Admin Guide**.
:::

## Get started

1. Sign in at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de) with GitHub.
2. Land in your workspace. Repositories sync from your organisation automatically.
3. Read the next finding on a pull request. Open the mentor when you want to think out loud.

Read on with [Getting started](./getting-started).
