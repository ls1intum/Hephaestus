Hephaestus evaluates each contribution against the practices your project defines. When something needs attention, it tells you directly — with feedback that adapts to your track record per practice.

![Hephaestus hero illustration](./img/overview/agile_hephaestus.png)

This guide is for anyone using Hephaestus at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de) — whether you're a student, mentor, or facilitator.

## The practice-aware loop

### Detect — practice findings on every contribution

- Each workspace owns a **practice catalog** — your standards, not generic rules.
- An AI agent evaluates each contribution against the catalog and produces **structured findings** with a verdict, severity, evidence, and a recommended action.
- Confirm, dispute, or dismiss any finding. Disputes are signal, not noise — they help us tune detection and the catalog.

### Coach — adaptive guidance, in context and in conversation

- Findings appear as **PR/MR comments and inline diff notes** where the work happens.
- The **conversational mentor (Heph)** is a reflection partner — surfacing your activity, helping you set goals, asking what is blocking you. It does not write code on your behalf.
- Tone adapts: concrete examples for newcomers, direct coaching for repeats, reflection prompts as you improve.

### Reflect — your own surfaces, scoped privately

- Your **profile** shows the contributions and practice signals scoped to you.
- A weekly **leaderboard** is available where the workspace has it enabled.
- Optional **weekly Slack digests** spotlight standout contributors and notable work.

:::info Workspace settings
Admins choose which repositories are in scope, assign workspace roles, and control what is sent to LLM providers.
:::

## Who should use this guide?

- Students and engineers participating in a Hephaestus-enabled course or project.
- Teaching assistants and facilitators who watch contributor progress and review practice findings.

:::tip Stay on track
This section is for end-user workflows. For deployment, architecture, or contribution guidance, jump to the **Contributor Guide** or **Admin Guide**.

## Quick start

1. Sign in with your GitHub account at [hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de](https://hephaestus.aet.cit.tum.de).
2. Join your course or team workspace. Repositories sync automatically from your organisation's GitHub App installation.
3. Open the **Mentor** tab to set your goals, then read findings on your pull requests as they appear.

Continue with [Getting started](./getting-started) for a hands-on walkthrough of the onboarding journey.
